Author

Due 4/13/2024

Introduction

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) and Data Visualization are closely tied concepts in data science.

They help us understand and interpret data.

We employ EDA to summarize the main characteristics of a dataset. Much of it will use data visualization methods prior to modeling.

You are already familiar with many methods and we will go through them and add more, but for this assignment, here is a catalog of many for reference.

https://datavizproject.com/

Part II Explore some data, generate a simple visualization and talk about it.

We will use the centenarians dataset from tidytuesday:

https://github.com/rfordatascience/tidytuesday/blob/master/data/2023/2023-05-30/readme.md

  1. Please create a histogram, a density and a boxplot of the age variable. You can use ggplot or something else. What is more important here is “the story”. If you had to use one of these graphics to talk about age in this dataset to your coworkers/supervisors/thesis advisor, which one would you prefer to use and why?

  2. Please create one new visualization of age and add a second variable. So for example, you could add gender and compare boxplots of age. Whatever you choose to do, keep the storytelling aspect in mind. Do some visualizations work better than others? Do they make talking about the relationship between age and second variable easier?

  3. Please video record your answers to A and B as if you were presenting your findings. I should be able to see the graphic you decided to talk about and you should be able to discuss briefly why you chose the one you presented and not one of the others.

Part III - Discover and explore data of your choosing, submit ONE simple visualization, and write about it and the process of arriving at it.

  • If you are currently working on your MASDS thesis, perhaps use its data for this part, if not, perhaps something you might use for your thesis or just something you like thinking/talking/writing about.

  • Data you cannot use: iris, diamonds, penguins, Titanic, anything from tidytuesday (unless your thesis advisor has already approved the use of any of those).

  • You may use ChatGPT or some other chatbot to help you.

  1. The visualization should be thesis quality (you can examine MASDS theses online for a sense of what that means, the bar is not high but I would hope you would aim high) Examples from the eScholarship Repository

  2. The visualization should contain a minimum of two features (variables, fields, whatever you like to call them). Example: in part II use we used age and gender.

  3. You should be able to write about what you tried (you do not need to show any of the rejects unless you want to for shock/surprise/contrast) and why you didn’t like it or didn’t choose it. There is no bad answer here, I want you to see/hear your internal decision making with respect to data visualization. Perhaps just a few sentences.

  4. You should be able to tell a brief story about the one visualization you chose for Part III. Perhaps a short paragraph.

  5. Please submit a single PDF with the graphic of B and your answers to C and D with it.
